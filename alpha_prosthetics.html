What we're building

We are creating Alpha Prosthetics, a venture dedicated to developing affordable, accessible, and durable prosthetic devices tailored to the needs of people with disabilities in Tanzania and other African countries. By utilizing innovative technologies such as 3D printing and modular design, we aim to significantly reduce the cost of prosthetic limbs while maintaining high quality and functionality. Our prosthetics are designed to withstand the diverse environmental conditions in Africa.

By establishing local production facilities and training programs, we intend to make these essential devices readily available, even in remote areas. Our holistic approach includes collaborating with local healthcare providers, NGOs, and government agencies to create a sustainable ecosystem that supports amputees from fitting to rehabilitation.

Why we're doing this

In Tanzania and across Africa, there is a significant shortage of prosthetic devices for people with disabilities. Financial constraints make prosthetics unaffordable for many amputees and their families. Limited healthcare infrastructure, including a scarcity of trained prosthetists and inadequate facilities, hinders access to prosthetic services. Many amputees, especially in rural areas, have little to no access to the necessary care and devices, leading to severe limitations in mobility and independence.

According to the World Health Organization, only 5-15% of people who require assistive devices in low- and middle-income countries have access to them. In Tanzania, many amputees live on the streets due to the lack of available prosthetics, highlighting the urgent need for affordable solutions. Our mission with Alpha Prosthetics is to empower these individuals by providing prosthetics that improve mobility, enhance quality of life, and foster social and economic inclusion.
